How might we help marketers discover and act on revenue opportunities — without requiring them to be data scientists?

The Problem

Users have to manually analyze data to find improvement opportunities at scale

Klaviyo sits on a massive "treasure trove" of 1st-party customer data, but unlocking insights is manual, time-consuming, and gated by marketing maturity. Regardless of scale, raw data does not equal revenue.

The Existing Solution

Generic suggestions failed to drive adoption

The previous experience relied on high-level account metrics to generate generic recommendations lacking specific customer context. Instead of enabling immediate action, suggestions often redirected users to external help documentation — breaking their focus and stalling momentum.

Speaking To Users

I interviewed 18 marketers across 9 industries to validate this problem

I conducted a listening tour to stress-test our hypothesis. Marketers expressed that current workflows are too technical — and without proper context, data becomes a burden rather than an asset, leading to analysis paralysis.

Design Strategy & Priorities

I pitched 3 strategies to move from a passive data repository to an active growth engine.

🔢

Make the numbers tell a story

Users are drowning in dense data rows without clear takeaways. We pivoted to summarizing high-level patterns and metrics to surface immediate value — replacing raw numbers with narrative context.

👀

Show the 'why' to earn trust

Raw data without context creates anxiety rather than clarity. We focused on increasing trust by backing every system suggestion with proven evidence, benchmarks, and transparent reasoning.

🏃

Make it easy to take action

Insight without a clear path forward is wasted. Every recommendation needed a one-click path to execution — closing the gap between opportunity identification and campaign activation.

Early Dashboard Concept

Abstracting performance into a gamified score

Early concept — composite health score (0–100) modeled on a credit report

Hypothesis: Summarizing complex account data with an abstract composite score would reduce cognitive load on marketers — similar to how a credit score distills financial health into a single actionable number.

I created a feature to give users an at-a-glance understanding of their marketing performance, anchored on a composite score and framing recommendations as opportunities to "gain points."

We Simplified the Data But Lost the Meaning

Early testing revealed a new problem

I stress-tested this experience with marketers and the feedback was consistent: the "Health Score" reduced visual clutter, but increased anxiety. The abstraction had severed the link that mattered most.

The Translation Gap

Users couldn't translate a "+12 point increase" into a business outcome. The score abstracted away the very numbers marketers use to justify decisions to their leadership.

The Validation Barrier

Users couldn't verify the source of the recommendation, so they hesitated to act. Without transparency, trust in the platform's intelligence eroded rather than grew.

Defining Dashboard Features

I designed three features to help marketers validate opportunities and act on them.

I replaced the abstract composite score with a projected revenue metric — a clear signal that users instinctively understand and can take directly to leadership.

Feature 01

Total Revenue Opportunity

To avoid false precision, I displayed estimates as revenue ranges backed by industry benchmarks. Acknowledging the potential margin of error demonstrated statistical rigor and built the transparency required for trust.

Feature 02

Proactive Recommendations

We packaged analysis into a single atomic unit — a card that reveals the specific data pattern (the "why"), quantifies the estimated revenue at stake (the value), and offers a direct CTA to execute the strategy. This removes friction between identifying an opportunity and capturing it.

Feature 03

Human-AI Validation Flows

We designed a "human-in-the-loop" workflow to mitigate the fear of blind automation. The AI acts as the production engine — selecting strategy and generating full emails, SMS, and imagery — but the user retains final authority. Marketers validate and configure every asset before it goes live, ensuring speed never compromises brand safety.

Final Designs

Redesigned Dashboard Experience

AI-Driven Insights

User goal: Browse opportunities and validate the logic and data behind each recommendation.

AI-Assisted Workflows

User goal: Act on insights immediately, speeding up workflows with AI-generated content and emails.

Tracking Model Performance

User goal: Understand how the AI's recommendations are performing over time and build confidence in the system.

By rooting each recommendation in peer data and framing results in revenue, we turned generic tasks into high-value opportunities marketers felt confident pursuing. This effectively bridged the gap between platform tasks and business goals — giving users a quantifiable target rooted in reality.
