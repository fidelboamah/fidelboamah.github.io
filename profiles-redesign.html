Overview

Redesigning the core profiles experience for troubleshooting

I led the redesign of Klaviyo's most legacy product area, Profiles. Inspired by the opportunity of a technical migration, I influenced a pivot from a mere 1:1 conversion of the profiles experience to a complete redesign — optimizing workflows and tools for troubleshooting. The work I did reduced support-related tickets by 20% and introduced new components and patterns to the design system.

The Problem

Engineering initiated a technical migration from Backbone.js to React

Technical Migration
{ }
Backbone
→
React

In January 2023, engineering kicked off a mandate to migrate Klaviyo's oldest frontend code from Backbone.js to React ahead of the company's IPO compliance push. The initial scope was a strict 1:1 conversion — rebuild what exists, exactly as it exists, just in new code.

The Problem

Imagine a product area that hasn't evolved since 2012

Profiles is the centralized repository for every piece of customer data Klaviyo holds — but the experience hadn't meaningfully changed since 2012. It was riddled with broken navigation paths, deprecated UI patterns, and workflows that no longer matched how marketers actually used the product. A straight 1:1 rebuild would have locked in over a decade of accumulated debt for years to come.

Design Strategy & Priorities

I led a 3-week vision sprint, developing data-driven ideas that aligned with marketers' goals

🧑🏿‍💻

User Archetype: The Troubleshooter

Someone who enters a customer profile to resolve issues, verify data accuracy, or confirm the status of a record.

User Priorities

🔍 Search

Marketers need workflows that help them quickly access key profile data without digging through nested menus.

💭 Understand

Marketers need a clear picture of a customer's relationship to the business at a glance.

The Business Case

Quantifying the Cost of Friction

I partnered with business analysts to quantify the operational drag created by the existing Profiles experience — translating design friction into hard numbers leadership could act on.

Pivot

From Migration to Redesign

Using the cost-of-support-tickets calculation, I made the case to leadership that a 20% reduction in tickets would pay for the redesign work within six months — shifting the project's scope from a pure 1:1 migration to a full redesign.

The Existing Solution

Profiles Homepage

The existing Profiles homepage created billing confusion — it was unclear whether counts referred to email or SMS profiles — and lacked any meaningful search functionality.

The Existing Solution

Customer Profile Page

The individual customer profile page suffered from information overload — roughly 50% of the data marketers needed for troubleshooting was buried below the fold.

Aligning Architecture With Marketing Intent

Design Strategies & Priorities

We prioritized the search experience and restructured the information hierarchy to surface the data marketers needed most — elevating consent status and contact details to the top of the page.

Final Design

Redesigned Dashboard Experience

Profile count redesign

Addressed billing concerns with a clear card layout and contextual tooltips explaining what each count represents.

Data table redesign

Added search and column filtering to the profiles table, reducing the steps to find a record from 7 clicks to 3.

Information Architecture

We locked the information architecture before designing layouts, elevating consent status in the hierarchy so it's immediately visible to anyone troubleshooting a profile.

Troubleshooting flow for consent

A streamlined flow lets marketers quickly verify a customer's consent status and history without leaving the profile page.

Impact

We improved profiles by aligning the experience with marketers' mental models

The redesign cut time-on-task by 31% and led to a 23% drop in support tickets related to the Profiles area.
